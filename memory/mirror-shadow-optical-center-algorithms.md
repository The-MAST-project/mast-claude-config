Algorithmic logic behind `src/imaging/mirror_shadow.py` and `src/imaging/optical_center.py` (MAST_unit).
Companion to the WIP status note [[mirror-shadow-detection-impl]] and the design [[unit-self-calibration-design]] §10 (pick-off stage) and §2 (optical center). Every non-obvious choice below was forced by testing on real frames in `D:/MAST/tmp/Samples/`.

## A. Shadow model (one object serves detect + mark + centerline)
The folding-mirror shadow is a **tilted band**, captured in `ShadowModel`: `angle` (long-axis orientation, rad from +x), `offset` (signed perpendicular distance of the centerline from image center), `umbra_half_width`, `penumbra_half_width`, `depth` (peak fractional flux deficit), `prominence` (trough SNR). The "long centerline" is simply `(angle, offset)`; masks and centerline endpoints are derived from it via the perpendicular coordinate `s(x,y) = -(x-cx)·sin(angle) + (y-cy)·cos(angle)` (band = where `|s-offset|` is small).

## B. Detection algorithm (`detect_mirror_shadow`)
1. **Deficit map.** `deficit = clip(1 - image/illum, 0, None)`. A *ratio* (not raw difference) so it's invariant to exposure/sky level. Stars are a flux *excess* → `1-excess<0` → clip to 0, so stars self-suppress and never look like shadow. With a retracted `reference=` frame, `illum=reference` (the cleaner mode); else `illum` is a fitted sky (next point).
2. **Illumination = sigma-clipped low-order 2D polynomial**, NOT a local `Background2D`. A local background with a box narrower than the (wide) mirror band would track the band and erase the very signal we want. The clip rejects stars (and, weakly, the band).
3. **Orientation by projection sweep (Radon-style).** For each candidate angle, bin the deficit onto the band-normal axis (`np.bincount` weighted) → a 1-D profile; the band is a trough. Score = `(peak - baseline)/noise` with `baseline=median`, `noise=1.4826·MAD` over *valid* bins (bins sampled across ≥30 % of the frame — drops sparse corners). Best-scoring angle wins. Projecting *along* the band averages out individual stars/ghosts. Sweep is full **±90°** (band orientation unknown; "usually not vertical" but can be). For speed the search runs on a **decimated** map (~4 s vs ~37 s on 3923×2771), then the winning angle is **re-measured at full resolution**.
4. **False-positive gates (all learned from real data).** (a) **min penumbra half-width** — a real mirror band is wide; rejects a 2-px bad-column / poly-edge sliver. (b) **edge margin** — the peak must sit away from the perpendicular extremes; a real edge-to-edge centerline crosses the field, and the poly *overshoots* near frame edges (creates spurious thin deficit ridges). (c) **valid-bin floor** above.
5. **Centerline = midpoint of the penumbra-to-penumbra run, NOT the deepest bin.** The cross-section is often **saddle-shaped** (two humps), so `argmax` would bias the axis sideways. Half-widths come from the umbra/penumbra threshold runs around the peak.
6. **Present test:** `depth-baseline ≥ min_depth (0.08)` AND `prominence ≥ min_prominence (5)` AND `penumbra_half_width ≥ min`. Always returns a `ShadowModel`; `.present` separates shadow-there from retracted, so the caller short-circuits.

## C. Darkening / leak-through removal (`darken_shadow`)
The mirror is **not fully opaque** → under the band you get *dimmed sky* plus *bright-star ghosts*. Its science is compromised regardless, so rather than un-vignette it we **replace the whole band with the sky that should be there**, erasing both the dip and the ghosts (the "mask before the pipeline" step; `model.mask()` is the boolean version). Logic:
- Refit the illumination poly with the **band excluded** (`_fit_illumination(exclude_mask=...)`). A wide deep band biases even a clipped poly downward; excluding it lets the poly interpolate the true sky across the gap.
- A low-order poly can't nail the *local* level, so measure a residual **offset correction** and the **pixel noise** in a thin **collar** hugging the band (dilated mask minus mask); noise via a high-pass (`data - median_filter`) so vignetting structure doesn't inflate it. Fill = `bg + offset + matched noise` → seamless.
- Verified on real frame: in-band max 8568→3808 (ghosts gone), level+noise match sky, no seam. Modes: sky-fill (default), `fill=np.nan` (mask-aware downstream), `fill=0` (hard blank), `include_penumbra=False`.

## D. Optical center (`find_optical_center`, coma-null) — recap
Coma elongates each star ~radially about the optical axis, growing ~linearly with field radius, so every star's **major-axis line passes through the center**. Fit = **weighted least-squares intersection** of those lines, in **normal-vector form** (numerically stable for all orientations; the old `tan(theta)` form blew up near vertical). **Weight by ellipticity** (round near-axis stars have noise-dominated orientation → low weight). **Iterative sigma-clip** on axis-to-center residuals. `middle_third` is a prior that the axis lies in the central third.

## E. The shadow ↔ optical-center coupling (the key logic)
**Observed failure:** on a shadowed frame, `find_optical_center` fit **5/5 sources that were leak-through ghosts inside the band** → center off by ~1 frame, residual RMS 126 px. Cause is a *compounding* of two things: ghosts get detected as sources, AND `middle_third=True` restricts sources to the central third — which is exactly where the (centered) pick-off shadow sits — so the only survivors are ghosts.
- **Mechanism added:** `find_optical_center(image, exclude_mask=...)` passes the mask to `detect_threshold`/`detect_sources`, so band pixels are never detected. (`image` now also accepts an array, so it composes with `darken_shadow` without a temp file.)
- **Decision — does optical-center finding *mandate* retracting the mirror?** Geometrically **no**: a centered band removes a stripe but 4-quadrant star coverage still constrains the 2-D convergence, so a *masked* fit can in principle recover the center on a star-rich field. **Operationally yes — mandate retraction.** Rationale: calibration is never concurrent with spectroscopy (the only reason to insert the mirror), the pick-off shadow sits *on the optical axis* = exactly the region of interest, and a masked fit's validity is conditional — so leaving the mirror in is risk for zero upside.
- **Therefore the real role of shadow detection here is a PRECONDITION GUARD, not a workaround:** run `detect_mirror_shadow` on the calibration frame; if `.present`, the mirror didn't actually retract → flag `calibration:stage-geometry` / abort, don't fit around it. The `exclude_mask` masking path stays as a quality-gated best-effort fallback only.

## F. Validation status (real frames, `D:/MAST/tmp/Samples/`)
**Detector validated only on DEEP shadows; clean-side NOT validated; a faint-shadow false-negative floor is now known (2026-06-23).**
- 15 independent **mirror-in** downsampled guide frames (the `…GuideMode` FIFFA shadow) → near-identical geometry (tilt +88/89°, offset −70±5 px, umbra ≈448, penumbra ≈570), depth 0.25–0.44, prominence 27–120. A fixed feature recovered identically 15× = strong robustness on deep shadows.
- `full-frame.fits` (8288×5644, a different camera/path) was first mis-read as CLEAR, but **Arie confirmed it DOES contain a shadow** — a genuine but very faint one (~4.4 % deep, near-vertical, ~col 3646). The detector **misses it (false negative)**: at ~4 % the band is *smaller than the frame's own ~5 % large-scale illumination structure* (gradient + a very bright star), so it's swamped. Verified single-image is at its limit here — lowering thresholds, higher-order/normalized poly (orders 2→8, residual stuck ~100 ADU), profile detrending, and star-suppressed edge/dip scoring all fail to make the real band win over spurious gradient features.
- **Consequences:** (a) still **no confirmed clean frame** → false-positive path remains unvalidated; (b) the gates (`min_depth=0.08`, `min_prominence=5`) are right for deep operational shadows but a deep↔faint↔clean ambiguity is unresolved.
- **Decision (Arie, 2026-06-23): leave as-is for now.** Current detector targets the deep operational shadows; the faint-shadow case is treated as an outlier. The robust fix when/if faint shadows prove operationally relevant is **reference/flat ratio mode** (`deficit = image/reference`, already supported) — the only way to isolate a ~4 % band from comparable vignetting, and the only safe way to raise sensitivity without a clean frame to bound false positives. A single-image penumbra-edge-pair detector is the alternative but is borderline at 4 % and unvalidatable without a clean frame.
- Perf note: full-res detect ≈17 s vs ≈4 s downsampled (∝ pixels).
