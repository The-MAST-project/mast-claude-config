Algorithmic logic behind `src/imaging/mirror_shadow.py` and `src/imaging/optical_center.py` (MAST_unit).
Companion to the WIP status note [[mirror-shadow-detection-impl]] and the design [[unit-self-calibration-design]] ¬ß10 (pick-off stage) and ¬ß2 (optical center). Every non-obvious choice below was forced by testing on real frames in `D:/MAST/tmp/Samples/`.

## A. Shadow model (one object serves detect + mark + centerline)
The folding-mirror shadow is a **tilted band**, captured in `ShadowModel`: `angle` (long-axis orientation, rad from +x), `offset` (signed perpendicular distance of the centerline from image center), `umbra_half_width`, `penumbra_half_width`, `depth` (peak fractional flux deficit), `prominence` (trough SNR). The "long centerline" is simply `(angle, offset)`; masks and centerline endpoints are derived from it via the perpendicular coordinate `s(x,y) = -(x-cx)¬∑sin(angle) + (y-cy)¬∑cos(angle)` (band = where `|s-offset|` is small).

## B. Detection algorithm (`detect_mirror_shadow`)
1. **Deficit map.** `deficit = clip(1 - image/illum, 0, None)`. A *ratio* (not raw difference) so it's invariant to exposure/sky level. Stars are a flux *excess* ‚Üí `1-excess<0` ‚Üí clip to 0, so stars self-suppress and never look like shadow. With a retracted `reference=` frame, `illum=reference` (the cleaner mode); else `illum` is a fitted sky (next point).
2. **Illumination = sigma-clipped low-order 2D polynomial**, NOT a local `Background2D`. A local background with a box narrower than the (wide) mirror band would track the band and erase the very signal we want. The clip rejects stars (and, weakly, the band).
3. **Orientation by projection sweep (Radon-style).** For each candidate angle, bin the deficit onto the band-normal axis (`np.bincount` weighted) ‚Üí a 1-D profile; the band is a trough. Score = `(peak - baseline)/noise` with `baseline=median`, `noise=1.4826¬∑MAD` over *valid* bins (bins sampled across ‚â•30 % of the frame ‚Äî drops sparse corners). Best-scoring angle wins. Projecting *along* the band averages out individual stars/ghosts. Sweep is full **¬±90¬∞** (band orientation unknown; "usually not vertical" but can be). For speed the search runs on a **decimated** map (~4 s vs ~37 s on 3923√ó2771), then the winning angle is **re-measured at full resolution**.
4. **False-positive gates (all learned from real data).** (a) **min penumbra half-width** ‚Äî a real mirror band is wide; rejects a 2-px bad-column / poly-edge sliver. (b) **edge margin** ‚Äî the peak must sit away from the perpendicular extremes; a real edge-to-edge centerline crosses the field, and the poly *overshoots* near frame edges (creates spurious thin deficit ridges). (c) **valid-bin floor** above.
5. **Centerline = midpoint of the penumbra-to-penumbra run, NOT the deepest bin.** The cross-section is often **saddle-shaped** (two humps), so `argmax` would bias the axis sideways. Half-widths come from the umbra/penumbra threshold runs around the peak.
6. **Present test:** `depth-baseline ‚â• min_depth (0.08)` AND `prominence ‚â• min_prominence (5)` AND `penumbra_half_width ‚â• min`. Always returns a `ShadowModel`; `.present` separates shadow-there from retracted, so the caller short-circuits.

## C. Darkening / leak-through removal (`darken_shadow`)
The mirror is **not fully opaque** ‚Üí under the band you get *dimmed sky* plus *bright-star ghosts*. Its science is compromised regardless, so rather than un-vignette it we **replace the whole band with the sky that should be there**, erasing both the dip and the ghosts (the "mask before the pipeline" step; `model.mask()` is the boolean version). Logic:
- Refit the illumination poly with the **band excluded** (`_fit_illumination(exclude_mask=...)`). A wide deep band biases even a clipped poly downward; excluding it lets the poly interpolate the true sky across the gap.
- A low-order poly can't nail the *local* level, so measure a residual **offset correction** and the **pixel noise** in a thin **collar** hugging the band (dilated mask minus mask); noise via a high-pass (`data - median_filter`) so vignetting structure doesn't inflate it. Fill = `bg + offset + matched noise` ‚Üí seamless.
- Verified on real frame: in-band max 8568‚Üí3808 (ghosts gone), level+noise match sky, no seam. Modes: sky-fill (default), `fill=np.nan` (mask-aware downstream), `fill=0` (hard blank), `include_penumbra=False`.

## D. Optical center (`find_optical_center`, coma-null) ‚Äî recap
Coma elongates each star ~radially about the optical axis, growing ~linearly with field radius, so every star's **major-axis line passes through the center**. Fit = **weighted least-squares intersection** of those lines, in **normal-vector form** (numerically stable for all orientations; the old `tan(theta)` form blew up near vertical). **Weight by ellipticity** (round near-axis stars have noise-dominated orientation ‚Üí low weight). **Iterative sigma-clip** on axis-to-center residuals. `middle_third` is a prior that the axis lies in the central third. ``image`` accepts a path or array; ``exclude_mask`` keeps regions (e.g. a shadow band) out of detection.

**Quality gates added 2026-06-23 (else it returns a confident-but-meaningless center):**
- **Radiality** (`min_radiality=0.15`): `radiality = <w¬∑cos 2(theta ‚àí theta_radial)>` over inliers, +1 = radial(coma) / 0 = random / ‚àí1 = tangential(field rotation/trailing). Stored on the result. Without it, least-squares of randomly-oriented lines just returns the star-distribution centroid (‚âà geometric center). Found via `full-frame.fits`: bright/off-axis stars there are *tangential* (mean axis-to-radial 58¬∞, radiality ‚âà ‚àí0.05) ‚Üí no coma ‚Üí now correctly `None`.
- **Min sources** (`min_sources=12`): radiality alone is fooled by a *few clustered* sources (e.g. 5 leak-through ghosts in a shadow band) ‚Äî they overfit a center that looks radial (sample1: radiality 0.87 on 5 ghosts, rms 126 px). A spread-source floor catches that.
- **Caveat:** the *pass* side is unvalidated ‚Äî no confirmed good-coma frame yet, so both thresholds (12, 0.15) are reasonable defaults but untuned (same gap as the shadow clean-frame).

## E. The shadow ‚Üî optical-center coupling (the key logic)
**Observed failure:** on a shadowed frame, `find_optical_center` fit **5/5 sources that were leak-through ghosts inside the band** ‚Üí center off by ~1 frame, residual RMS 126 px. Cause is a *compounding* of two things: ghosts get detected as sources, AND `middle_third=True` restricts sources to the central third ‚Äî which is exactly where the (centered) pick-off shadow sits ‚Äî so the only survivors are ghosts.
- **Mechanism added:** `find_optical_center(image, exclude_mask=...)` passes the mask to `detect_threshold`/`detect_sources`, so band pixels are never detected. (`image` now also accepts an array, so it composes with `darken_shadow` without a temp file.)
- **Decision ‚Äî does optical-center finding *mandate* retracting the mirror?** Geometrically **no**: a centered band removes a stripe but 4-quadrant star coverage still constrains the 2-D convergence, so a *masked* fit can in principle recover the center on a star-rich field. **Operationally yes ‚Äî mandate retraction.** Rationale: calibration is never concurrent with spectroscopy (the only reason to insert the mirror), the pick-off shadow sits *on the optical axis* = exactly the region of interest, and a masked fit's validity is conditional ‚Äî so leaving the mirror in is risk for zero upside.
- **Therefore the real role of shadow detection here is a PRECONDITION GUARD, not a workaround:** run `detect_mirror_shadow` on the calibration frame; if `.present`, the mirror didn't actually retract ‚Üí flag `calibration:stage-geometry` / abort, don't fit around it. The `exclude_mask` masking path stays as a quality-gated best-effort fallback only.

## G. Optical center ‚Äî coma IS present and now recovered (2026-06-23, CORRECTED)

Two bugs hid it: (a) **ellipticity weighting** gave faint noise-elongated stars equal say and washed out the few bright coma stars; (b) **`middle_third=True` filtered SOURCES to the central third**, excluding the off-axis edge stars that carry coma. **Spin-1 implemented (2026-06-24).** Centroid-vs-peak offset per source: `o = (xcentroid,ycentroid) - (maxval_xindex,maxval_yindex)` from the SourceCatalog; `theta_cp = atan2(o)`, weighted by `flux * |o|` (sub-pixel `|o|<0.5` is peak-quantization noise, dropped). Key finding: spin-1 is a much **cleaner coma CONFIRMATION** ‚Äî weighted radiality `<cos(theta_cp - radial)>` = **0.90‚Äì0.95** on real coma (vs spin-2 ellipse 0.61‚Äì0.78), and it confirms the *outward sense* a headless ellipse can't ‚Äî BUT it's a **worse center ESTIMATOR** (a centroid-peak line-intersection fit scatters 1013 px vs the ellipse's 300 px; offsets ~1‚Äì2 px are too noisy to *locate* the center). So: **ellipse fits the center, spin-1 gates/cross-checks.** `OpticalCenterResult.radiality_spin1` added; the gate uses spin-1 when ‚â•`min_sources` resolved offsets exist, else falls back to spin-2. Separation is much better than spin-2: clean coma 0.90+, trailed/marginal full-frame 0.41, no-coma ~0 ‚Äî so use `radiality_spin1` (‚âà0.7+ = clean) for per-frame quality selection.
